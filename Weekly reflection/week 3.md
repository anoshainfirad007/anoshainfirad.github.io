## 🌟 Week 3:Personal Branding 

### 📖 Topic Covered:
This lecture focused on the importance of personal branding for Computer Science students and the methods to build it. It emphasized creating a distinct professional identity through platforms like LinkedIn and intentional professional networking

### 🧠 What I Learned:
I learned that personal branding is the intentional process of managing my professional identity to clearly communicate my skills, values, and career goals to potential employers.

It includes a combination of technical skills, project work, professional behavior, and online presence. A strong personal brand requires balancing technical expertise with a professional attitude and active visibility on digital platforms.

### 💡 Key Insight:
A key realization for me is that a university degree alone is not enough in today’s competitive tech industry. My digital presence often creates the first impression before I even get an interview.

Employers and recruiters often check platforms like LinkedIn for professional profiles and GitHub for technical skills and project quality.

I also learned that networking is not about directly asking for jobs, but about building meaningful and long-term professional relationships.

### 🌍 Real-life Application:
In the future, I will actively build my personal brand by improving my LinkedIn profile with a professional photo, a clear headline, and a strong “About” section that reflects my career interests.

I will also use GitHub as a technical portfolio by uploading well-organized projects, writing clear README files, and maintaining clean, well-documented code.

While networking, I will focus on starting respectful and professional conversations, rather than directly requesting job opportunities.
